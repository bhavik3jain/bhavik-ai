## KEY MACRO DRIVERS

### 1. The Iran War (Primary Catalyst)
The US-Iran conflict is now in its **fifth week** and shows no sign of quick resolution. The most consequential development: the **closure of the Strait of Hormuz on March 4**, which handles ~20% of global oil shipments. This has stranded oil and LNG exports and sent energy prices surging. Investors who had hoped for a quick resolution are growing increasingly uneasy.

### 2. Oil Prices — At War Highs
- **Brent Crude:** ~$105.85/barrel (as of March 26 — up $6.10 overnight and ~$32 above year-ago levels)
- Crude crossed the **$100/barrel** psychological threshold in mid-March and has held above it
- Oil futures jumped 6.76% at one point during peak volatility
- Energy is the **only S&P 500 sector posting gains** over the recent period (+1.5% weekly)

### 3. Treasury Yields — Ratcheting Higher
- **10-Year Treasury Yield:** ~4.41%, near **8-month highs**
- Yields are rising because oil-driven inflation is forcing the market to reprice Fed expectations — previously anticipated rate **cuts** are now being replaced with rate **hike** probabilities
- Higher yields = higher discount rates = pressure on equity valuations, especially long-duration tech

### 4. Dollar Index
- DXY holding near **100**, tracking a weekly gain of ~0.3%
- Dollar is benefiting from safe-haven flows and repriced Fed hawkishness
- Persistent geopolitical uncertainty continues to support USD

### 5. Stagflation Risk
The Iran oil shock has reignited **stagflation fears** — slowing growth + accelerating inflation. Key indicators:
- University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment: **53.3** in March — a level historically associated with either an active recession or its immediate approach
- Moody's AI Recession Model: **49%** probability (one point below the 50% threshold that has preceded every US recession in the past 80 years)
- J.P. Morgan Recession Probability: **35%**

## MARKET CONTEXT & WHAT TO WATCH

### The Correction Scorecard
- Dow: In correction (>10% off peak) — just entered
- Nasdaq: In correction — entered Thursday March 26
- S&P 500: ~7% off highs — approaching but not yet in official correction

### Historical Note on Corrections
Every time Moody's AI recession model crossed 50%, a recession followed within a year. At 49%, the model is at an inflection point. Historically, when the Nasdaq enters a correction, the market tends to recover — but the timeline and magnitude vary widely depending on whether the catalyst is resolved.

### Fed Watch
- Current Fed Funds Rate target: **3.5%–3.75%**
- Markets had priced in 50bps of cuts for 2026
- **The Iran war + oil shock is flipping this narrative** — rate hike probability is being priced back in
- Any Fed commentary this week will be closely watched

### Iran War — Key Question
The conflict is the single most important variable in the market right now. Watch for:
- Any diplomatic signals or ceasefire talks
- Strait of Hormuz reopening/closure updates
- Iran oil export disruption estimates vs. what's priced into crude

### Seasonality Note
April begins the final month of the "best six months" for stocks (October–April). Historically, April has been the **#2 best month** for the Dow, averaging +1.8% since 1950. Bulls are hoping seasonality provides a floor — but geopolitical risk is dominating.
