### Key Catalysts Today

- **Middle East / Iran Oil Shock**: U.S. and Israeli airstrikes on Iran (late February) cut off ~20% of global oil supply. Crude is spiking toward $120/barrel. This is the single largest macro driver in the market right now — every U.S. recession since WWII (except COVID) was preceded by a fuel price spike of this magnitude.
- **Recession Probability Rising Fast**: Moody's AI recession model sits at 49% — approaching the 50% threshold that has historically preceded a recession within 12 months. GDP revised down from 1.4% to 0.7%. February jobs report shocked: -92,000 jobs vs +59,000 expected; unemployment now at 4.4%.
- **S&P 500 Down ~7.4% for March**: On pace for worst month since March 2025. Large-cap tech (Mag 7) driving the bulk of losses. Dow is in correction territory, Nasdaq entered correction the prior week. Shiller P/E at 37.5 (vs. 21.3 average at prior recession peaks) — valuation risk remains high.

## Today's Themes

- **Oil Shock / Geopolitical Risk Drive Everything**: The Iran conflict is the primary market driver. With crude approaching $120/bbl and 20% of global supply disrupted, energy stocks (XOM, CVX, OXY, XLE) are the clearest momentum longs. Meanwhile, rising energy costs are crushing consumer discretionary names and feeding stagflation fears. Consider: long XLE / energy names, short XLY / consumer-facing discretionary.

- **Tech / Mag 7 Distribution Accelerating**: Meta, Amazon, Microsoft all saw -2% to -4% single-day moves on Friday. QQQ is down ~3.5% on the week. Rising 10Y yields (4.44%) compress growth multiples, and recession fears undermine forward earnings estimates for AI hyperscalers. The breadth data (577 Nasdaq new lows) confirms this is not just a top-name rotation but broad tech selling. Consider: puts on QQQ or individual Mag 7 names; or short-term bear spreads. Options premiums are elevated (VIX 31) — debit spreads are preferred over naked longs.

- **VIX Spike = Elevated Premiums, But Directional Clarity**: VIX at 31 makes buying outright puts expensive, but the directional thesis is clear. This environment is ideal for selling covered calls on any remaining long positions, or using bear put spreads to cap debit cost. If VIX spikes further toward 35–40, look for mean-reversion bounces as short-term oversold signals emerge — but the trend is your friend until breadth stabilizes.
